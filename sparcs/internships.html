Siena College CS Early Internships - Summer 2015

In the summer after their freshman year, six students will be selected from the students who have completed the first-year CS sequence (CSIS110 and CSIS120). They will spend six weeks (5/17-6/26) working on a real-world project for New York State Office of Information Technology Services (NYS ITS), the New York State Senate Technology Department, or Siena College’s Department of Information Technology Services. Interns will work in pairs on projects, and Siena faculty will serve as mentors and provide technical guidance.

Examples of the types of projects the students may work on include:

  • Researching and experimenting with new technologies.
  • Testing and evaluating software tools and applications.
  • Designing and creating web sites.
  • Prototyping user interfaces using tools like Flinto or Pop.
  • Requirements gathering.
  • Writing programs to analyze data sets or massage data into proper format.
  • Developing databases.
  • Managing and maintaining computers and providing technical support to users.

All student interns will receive a summer stipend of $1890 and non-local students will also be provided with an additional stipend to cover campus room and board. Students may choose to earn Siena credits for the internship, although normal summer tuition fees will apply. To apply, complete the online application by Friday, February 20, 2015.
